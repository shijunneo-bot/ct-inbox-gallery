What it is
App Inbox and Web Inbox are CleverTap's persistent message centres: messages that stay on the device or in a web panel, unlike a push that disappears.
Templates
Four app templates (Simple, Icon, Carousel with content, Carousel image-only), plus a custom key-value path for your own UI.
Shelf life
App Inbox TTL is 1 to 60 days (default 2) or Infinite. Web Inbox shows 5 to 50 messages (default 15).
Design control
SDK templates style via a config; full pixel control uses custom key-value while CleverTap keeps delivery and stats.

03 · Build vs buy

More design freedom is a lane, not an exit.

When a client says "we want full control of the look," the instinct is to think they must leave CleverTap and build their own inbox. They rarely need to. It is a spectrum, and the middle lane keeps the orchestration while giving the design team its canvas.

Lane 1 · Full buy

Use the SDK templates

CleverTap renders the inbox. You configure it.
  • Four ready templates: Simple, Icon, Carousel with content, Carousel image-only
  • Styled via a config (title, background, tab colours, labels), not free HTML
  • Fastest to ship; zero custom UI work
Trade-off: layout is fixed to the template. Good when speed matters more than pixels.
Start here
Lane 2 · Hybrid

Custom key-value, your UI

CleverTap delivers. Your app renders its own cell.
  • Message rides as custom key-value pairs (shared KV, SDK 5.2.0+)
  • Your design system, your layout, pixel-level control
  • You keep segmentation, personalization, TTL, and stats
The sweet spot: full design freedom without giving up the orchestration engine. Verify the exact SDK gate with your PM.
Lane 3 · Full build

Your own inbox, end to end

CleverTap only triggers. You own everything after.
  • Post Action Webhook fires to your endpoint on send
  • You build storage, TTL, read-state sync, render, and analytics
  • Maximum freedom, maximum invisible plumbing to maintain
Trade-off: you rebuild persistence, read-state, and stats that Lane 1 and 2 give you for free.

The honest read for a client: the jump from Lane 2 to Lane 3 buys very little extra design freedom and adds a lot of engineering you now own forever. Most "we need our own inbox" conversations end at Lane 2.

04 · Push + inbox

Pair the buzz with the memory.

A push and an inbox message are not rivals; they are a relay. The push gets attention in the moment. The inbox keeps a copy for when the person actually opens the app. In a push campaign, two switches do this, and your note is exactly right: a message can be linked or triggered from either one.

Switch A · in the push editor

App Inbox Delivery

Tick it inside a push campaign and the same message persists in the inbox with its own colours and its own Inbox TTL (Limited or Infinite). The push fires now; the inbox copy is there on the next open.

Send push→App Inbox Delivery on→Copy persists (own TTL)
Switch B · in the push editor

Post Action Webhook

Tick it and the campaign fires to an endpoint you own. Use it to populate your own message store or your own inbox. Combined with custom key-value pairs on the payload, this is the trigger for a fully custom build.

Send push→Post Action Webhook→Your endpoint / your inbox

Note on timing: App Inbox is session-based, so the persisted copy appears on the next app launch or session, not the instant the push lands. That is the point, the inbox is populated for when they come back.

05 · Decide

Inbox, push, or native display?

Three surfaces, three jobs. The quick rule: if it has shelf life, it belongs in the inbox. If it needs to interrupt right now, it is a push. If it lives inside a screen, it is native display.

07 · How stats work

What the inbox reports back.

App Inbox stats read a little differently from push, so it is worth knowing the formulas before a client asks. Note that CTR is measured against Viewed, not Sent.

Sent
Notifications sent to devices
Viewed
Devices that received it · Viewed / Sent
Clicks
Clicks on the message · Clicks / Sent
CTR
Clickthrough · Click / Viewed
Converted
Users who converted after receiving

On top of these, the stats page shows message trends (daily, weekly, monthly), a conversion and revenue view (incremental and boosted revenue), a conversion funnel by OS, errors, and control group. Each message stored on the device is counted as a user event.

08 · FAQ

Twenty questions, straight answers.

The mechanics and the styling, in plain language. Where availability depends on your plan or SDK version, it says so rather than guessing.

How long does an App Inbox message live on the device?
You set a Time to Live from 1 to 60 days (default 2 days), or choose Infinite so it never auto-removes. TTL counts from campaign sent time. Pick a life that matches the job: a coupon valid for a month wants a long TTL, a weekend flash sale wants a day or two.
Does the message appear the instant I send it?
App Inbox messages are session-based: they are fetched on the next app launch or new session. In a Push + App Inbox campaign the push arrives instantly, but the inbox copy shows up on the next open. That is by design, so the inbox is populated when the app is active.
How do unread counts and badges work?
The SDK exposes total and unread counts (getInboxMessageCount and getInboxMessageUnreadCount on Android, equivalents on iOS). You mark a message read when it is seen, and repaint your badge on the inbox-updated callback. The unread dot is driven by that read state.
Is delivery per-user or per-segment?
Both. You target a segment (past behaviour, live behaviour, or a custom list) like any channel, and personalization plus custom key-value make each user's inbox look different. One user's inbox is not another's.
What media and sizes are supported?
Image (jpg, png, gif) up to 500KB, video (mp4) up to 50MB, audio (mp3) up to 5MB, at 16:9 or 1:1. For video, keep an audio track present (add a silent track if needed).
Can each CTA deep-link somewhere different?
Yes. A message carries up to two CTAs, each an Open URL (separate iOS and Android URLs) or a custom key-value action. Tapping the message body itself returns button index -1, so you can route that too.
How is App Inbox set up versus Web Inbox?
App Inbox needs the mobile SDK (3.4.0+; KV buttons 3.6.1 Android / 3.7.1 iOS; shared KV across messages 5.2.0+) and a place to show the inbox UI. Web Inbox needs the Web SDK plus an Element ID: you give CleverTap a DOM node id in Setup and the bell, badge, and slide-in panel mount there.
What is a custom key-value inbox, and when do I use it?
If you want a fully custom inbox look, you keep CleverTap for delivery, segmentation, TTL, and stats, and pass the message as custom key-value pairs (SDK 5.2.0+). Your app reads the payload and renders your own UI. This is the middle path between the ready-made SDK templates and building an inbox from scratch. Verify the exact SDK gate for your build with your PM.
How do read and delivered stats work?
App Inbox stats report Sent (messages sent to devices), Viewed (devices that received it; Viewed/Sent), Clicks (Clicks/Sent), CTR (Click/Viewed), Converted users, plus errors and control group. There are trend, conversion, and revenue views. Each stored inbox message is counted as a user event.
Can a push and an inbox message be sent together?
Yes. In a push campaign you can tick App Inbox Delivery so the same message persists in the inbox with its own colours and Inbox TTL. The push grabs attention now; the inbox keeps a copy for when they open the app.
Can I trigger my own inbox or external system from a campaign?
Yes, through the Post Action Webhook on a push campaign: it fires to your endpoint so you can populate your own message store or your own inbox. Combined with custom key-value pairs on the payload, this is the build-your-own path.
Can App Inbox be triggered by an API event?
For a standalone App Inbox campaign, a live-action message will not deliver if the qualifying event arrives via API; to trigger via API you configure it through a Journey. This limit does not apply to Push + App Inbox campaigns. Confirm current behaviour with your PM before relying on it.
How do I sort messages into tabs?
Add Message Tags to a message; the tags map to tabs in your inbox UI (you name the tabs when you configure the inbox). Untagged messages fall into the default 'All' bucket. Tabs are optional.
Can I personalize inbox content?
Yes. Title, body, and CTA URLs accept {{personalization}} tokens and Liquid @tags, and you can attach Recommendations to generate per-user content. You can also A/B test, split-deliver, or vary by user property.
How big is the Web Inbox panel?
The default web panel is about 392 by 546 pixels on desktop, and takes full width and height on mobile. You choose how many messages it shows (5 to 50, default 15) and style the panel, header, tabs, cards, buttons, and badge from the dashboard.
How much design control do I actually have?
With the SDK templates you style through a config (title, background, tab colours, tab labels on app; a full colour set on web), not free HTML. For pixel-level control you use the custom key-value path and render your own UI. So 'more design freedom' does not mean leaving CleverTap; it means moving one lane along the spectrum.
What happens to messages after TTL expires?
They are automatically removed from the device once the TTL passes (counted from sent time). Infinite TTL messages stay until you remove them. Choose TTL to match how long the message stays useful.
Does the inbox work offline?
The messages already fetched to the device are available offline, since App Inbox persists locally. New messages are fetched on the next session when the device is online.
Which plan or packaging includes App Inbox and Web Inbox?
Channel availability depends on your CleverTap plan and packaging, which changes over time. Treat this as Verify with PM rather than a fixed answer, and check your project's entitlements before you promise a client.
Where does the CSM lane stop on all this?
You own the value story: which use cases, what shelf life, which design treatment, what outcome to prove. SDK setup, identity, and payload debugging sit with the customer's engineers and CleverTap support; commercial close and packaging sit with the AE. Bring the play, route the plumbing.
If it has shelf life, it belongs in the inbox.
